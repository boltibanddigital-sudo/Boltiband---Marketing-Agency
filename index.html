FAQs

Common Meta Ads questions.

How much budget do I need to start Meta Ads? +
We recommend a minimum of ₹15,000–₹25,000/month in ad spend to get enough data for meaningful optimisation. Management fees are separate.
Do you handle creatives (videos, images)? +
We write detailed creative briefs your team or a freelancer can execute. Our focus is strategy, targeting, and performance — not video production.
What is CAPI and why does it matter? +
CAPI (Conversions API) is server-side tracking that sends conversion data directly from your server to Meta — bypassing iOS14 browser restrictions. Without it, you lose 30–60% of your conversion signals.
How long before I see results? +
Most campaigns exit the learning phase in 7–14 days. Meaningful CPL improvement typically happens within 3–5 weeks. Unlike SEO, Meta can generate leads within 48 hours of going live.
Do you manage both Facebook and Instagram? +
Yes — both run through Meta Ads Manager. We allocate budget across placements based on where your audience converts most efficiently. Placement decisions are data-driven.

FAQs

Common CRO questions.

How much traffic do I need for CRO to work? +
You need enough traffic to reach statistical significance — typically 500–1,000 conversions per variant per test. For lower-traffic sites, we focus on qualitative methods first (heatmaps, session recordings, UX review) and make high-confidence changes before running formal A/B tests.
How long does a CRO test take to run? +
Most tests run for 2–4 weeks to collect enough data across different traffic patterns and days of the week. We never call a test early — even if one variant looks like it's winning — until we reach 95%+ statistical confidence.
Do you redesign the whole page or make targeted changes? +
Both, depending on what the data shows. Sometimes a single headline change doubles conversions. Sometimes the page structure itself is the problem. We let user behaviour data guide the scope — never our assumptions about what looks nice.
Can CRO run alongside our existing ad campaigns? +
Absolutely — and it's most powerful when it does. Improving your landing page conversion rate means every rupee you spend on ads delivers more results. A 40% lift in CVR is the same as cutting your CPL by 40%, without touching your bids.
What if a test produces no clear winner? +
An inconclusive test is still valuable — it tells us that particular variable isn't the bottleneck. We document the learning, move on to the next highest-impact hypothesis, and continue the cycle. No test is wasted.
Will this work with our existing website platform? +
Yes — CRO tools like Hotjar, Clarity, and VWO work across all major platforms including WordPress, Webflow, Shopify, custom builds, and landing page builders. We assess your setup during onboarding and confirm compatibility before starting.

Most GA4 setups we audit are tracking less than 70% of actual conversions — missing events, double-counting sessions, or firing tags incorrectly. "Set up" and "set up correctly" are very different things. We audit first and show you exactly what's missing before starting any work.
Do you need access to our website code? +
Usually not — Google Tag Manager handles most tracking changes without touching your code. We'll need GTM admin access and GA4 property access. For custom events (e.g. SPA apps or e-commerce data layers), we may need brief developer collaboration.
What's in the monthly performance reports? +
Each report covers: channel performance vs previous period, conversion trends, top and bottom performing campaigns, funnel drop-off points, and 3–5 specific recommendations for the next 30 days. Written in plain English — no jargon, no vanity metrics.
Can you connect our CRM data to GA4? +
Yes — and this is one of the highest-value things we do. By importing qualified lead and revenue data from your CRM back into GA4, we can show which campaigns and keywords actually drove customers — not just clicks. This makes every future ad decision far more accurate.
Is this a one-time setup or an ongoing retainer? +
We offer both. The initial setup is a one-time engagement (7–14 days). Ongoing retainers include monthly reports, dashboard maintenance, new event tracking as your site evolves, and quarterly deep-dive analysis. Most clients start with setup and move to ongoing.
Which channels can you track and attribute? +
All of them — Google Ads, Meta Ads, organic search, email, direct, referral, and any other channel you run. We set up cross-channel attribution models so you see how channels work together across the full customer journey, not just last-click credit.
